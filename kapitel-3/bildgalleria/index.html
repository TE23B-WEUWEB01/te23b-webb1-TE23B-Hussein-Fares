NASA's Galileo spacecraft

NASA's Galileo spacecraft took this image of Earth's Moon on Dec. 7, 1992, on its way to explore the Jupiter system in 1995-97. The distinct bright ray crater at the bottom of the image is the Tycho impact basin.

2017 Total Solar Eclipse

During the total solar eclipse, the Sun’s corona, only visible during the total eclipse, is shown as a crown of white flares from the surface. The red spots called Bailey's beads occurs where the moon grazes by the Sun and the rugged lunar limb topography allows beads of sunlight to shine through in some areas as photographed from NASA Armstrong’s Gulfstream III.

Buzz Aldrin Stands Beside the U.S. Flag on the Moon

Astronaut Edwin "Buzz" Aldrin poses for a photograph beside the U.S. flag during an Apollo 11 extravehicular activity (EVA) on the lunar surface.
